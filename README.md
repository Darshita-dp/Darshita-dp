## About

> **I build business-facing data and software products that move from raw data to operational action.**

I work across the full delivery chain: **SQL data models, Python ETL pipelines, Power BI dashboards, React interfaces, FastAPI services, and workflow automation**. My focus is healthcare operations—especially revenue cycle, claims and denials, patient access, scheduling, and decision support.

What makes my profile different is the combination of **analytics depth and software execution**. I do not stop at reporting what happened. I build systems that help teams decide **what to do next**, whether that means prioritizing a claim, escalating a payer, contacting a high-risk patient, or filling an open appointment slot.

## Featured Healthcare Products

<details open>
<summary><b>Healthcare Revenue Cycle Command Center</b></summary>
<br/>

> An end-to-end healthcare revenue cycle analytics and workflow platform that helps teams identify revenue risk and prioritize the next claim to work.

| Dimension | Delivery |
|:---|:---|
| **Business Problem** | Claim denials, payer delays, aging A/R, and fragmented follow-up decisions |
| **Data Foundation** | PostgreSQL star schema with 6,000 claims, 1,200 patients, 110 providers, 9 payers, 7 dimensions, and 5 fact tables |
| **Analytics Layer** | 20+ SQL analyses covering denial patterns, payer performance, A/R aging, recovery tracking, and operational KPIs |
| **Product Layer** | FastAPI services, a 6-page React work queue, and a 5-page Power BI executive reporting layer |
| **Decision Support** | Explainable 0–100 claim-priority score with business-readable drivers |
| **Innovation** | Revenue recovery simulator that estimates the impact of working prioritized claims |
| **Repository** | [View the project](https://github.com/Darshita-dp/Healthcare-Revenue-Cycle-Command-Center) |

The platform connects analytics with daily operations. Instead of showing only historical denial rates, it converts claims data into prioritized work queues, escalation alerts, and revenue-recovery scenarios so staff can act on the most important cases first.

**Stack:** `Python` `PostgreSQL` `SQL` `FastAPI` `React` `TypeScript` `Power BI` `DAX` `Docker`

</details>

<br/>

<details open>
<summary><b>Patient Access & Appointment No-Show Automation Platform</b></summary>
<br/>

> A patient-access decision-support platform that predicts no-show risk, recommends staff actions, and helps refill released appointment capacity.

| Dimension | Delivery |
|:---|:---|
| **Business Problem** | Missed appointments, wasted provider capacity, delayed patient access, and manual waitlist workflows |
| **Data Foundation** | Full ETL and feature-engineering pipeline with operational scheduling, clinic, provider, reminder, and waitlist data |
| **Predictive Analytics** | 0.72 ROC-AUC on temporal holdout; top 20% risk band captures 45% of no-shows at approximately 2× base-rate precision |
| **Product Layer** | 13 API endpoints, 8 React operational views, and a 7-page Power BI design |
| **Automation** | Recommended-action engine for calls, targeted SMS, escalation, and transportation checks |
| **Capacity Recovery** | Waitlist-matching engine ranks replacement candidates for released and open slots |
| **Repository** | [View the project](https://github.com/Darshita-dp/patient-access-noshow-automation-platform) |

The product moves beyond prediction. Each risk score is translated into a concrete staff action with a plain-language reason, while the waitlist engine helps operations teams recover capacity before an open slot expires.

**Stack:** `Python` `Scikit-learn` `PostgreSQL` `FastAPI` `React` `Power BI` `Power Automate`

</details>
